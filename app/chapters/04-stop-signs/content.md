A stop sign costs **$600**. A painted crosswalk costs **$500–$1,500**. Three of each — at **20th & Dearborn**, **20th & Charles**, and **the park path to the station** — would cost **under $6,500** and take **a few days of crew work**. Crosswalks at stop-controlled intersections reduce pedestrian conflicts by up to **40%**.

Judkins Park Station was originally planned to open in **2020**. Construction screw-ups — including a defective I-90 track bed that had to be rebuilt *twice* — pushed the opening to **March 2026**, a **six-year delay**. SDOT had *six extra years* to install stop signs and crosswalks on the streets surrounding this station. Instead, they installed **dozens** of [Restricted Parking Zone signs](https://www.seattle.gov/transportation/projects-and-programs/programs/parking-program/judkins-park) while a stop sign *planned* for **20th & Weller** — after a student was hit — still isn't in the ground. Every RPZ sign went up before a single new stop sign. They had the crews, the budget, and **six years of bonus time** to protect **pedestrians** — and chose to protect **parking spots**.
