A stop sign costs **$600**. A painted crosswalk costs **$500–$1,500**. Three of each — at **20th & Dearborn**, **20th & Charles**, and **the park path to the station** — would cost **under $6,500** and take **a few days of crew work**. Crosswalks at stop-controlled intersections reduce pedestrian conflicts by up to **40%**.

Judkins Park Station was originally planned to open in 2020. With the official opening date approaching in March 2026, SDOT had six extra years to install stop signs and crosswalks on the streets surrounding this station. Instead, we got dozens of Restricted Parking Zone signs while a stop sign planned for 20th & Weller — after a student was hit — still isn't in the ground. Every RPZ sign went up before a single new stop sign. They had the crews, the budget, and six years of bonus time to protect pedestrians — and chose to protect parking spots
