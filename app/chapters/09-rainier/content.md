SDOT's [Near-Term Crossing Improvements](https://www.seattle.gov/transportation/projects-and-programs/current-projects/i-90_judkins-park-station-near-term-crossing-improvements) project delivered real safety gains at the **bottom** of Judkins Park Station — three I-90 on/off ramps along **Rainier Ave S** now have **raised crosswalks** and **rectangular rapid flashing beacons**. Drivers who once blew through crosswalks at highway speed now face physical traffic calming and high-visibility signals.

This is exactly the kind of infrastructure that works. FHWA data shows raised crosswalks cut pedestrian crashes by **45%**, and RRFBs push driver yielding from 18% to over 80%. The Rainier side proves SDOT *can* build safe crossings near this station — **when it chooses to**.
